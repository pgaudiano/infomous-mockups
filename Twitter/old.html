Infomous Mockups for Twitter

Infomous is a cool new way to navigate online information through interactive text clouds. Infomous can summarize and display text from many sources, including RSS feeds and Twitter Feeds.

How could Twitter use Infomous?

Twitter has established itself as the dominant platform for real-time information exchange. However, the text-based nature of Twitter can make consumption of that information challenging. Infomous can support Twitter users by providing a quick, interactive, visual snapshot of Tweets.

Here are two examples of how Twitter could use Infomous to support its user base (links open in a new window/tab):

  • Embed an Infomous cloud in a user's Twitter home
  • Embed an Infomous cloud to show Twitter search results
Note: in the mockups above, the clouds are based on the tweets at the moment you view the page, while the rest of the page is static. Hence the tweets seen on the left of the page will probably not reflect those seen in the cloud.

Why would Twitter want to use Infomous?

  1. Infomous clouds provide a visual snapshot that makes it easier for users to visualize and navigate tweets.
  2. Infomous clouds can be used to deliver unobtrusive, contextually relevant text advertising similar to search engine advertising, but based on dynamic content of interest to your users.

We believe Infomous will become a dominant navigation tool, and that it will revolutionize the way advertisement is delivered. We would love to collaborate with a leading, innovative company like Twitter.

Additional Infomous features

  • Infomous clouds are fully customizable: size, colors, number of words, feeds, ... Twitter could choose to control customization or give its users some flexibility in their own clouds.
  • Infomous runs as SaaS, hosted on Amazon EC2 (which, in spite of recent issues, is quite robust).
  • Infomous can be easily embedded, requires virtually no "installation"
  • The nature and quality of the words, as well as the links, can be fully filtered or manipulated to allow for different types of user experience. For example, we could add a "chronology" feature allowing users to visualize tweets over a specific date range.
